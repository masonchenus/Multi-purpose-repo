## 7. Cookies, Tracking, and Similar Technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies to enhance your experience, understand user behavior, and personalize content. This section explains these technologies and your choices.

### 7.1 What Are Cookies?

**Cookie Basics:**
- Small text files stored on your device by your browser
- Sent to our servers with every request from your device
- Store information like preferences, login status, and browsing history
- Persist across sessions (unless they're session-only cookies)
- Do not execute code or spread viruses

**Types of Cookies:**

**Essential/Functional Cookies:**
- Required for Services to function properly
- Enable core features like login and account management
- Do not track you across websites
- Cannot be disabled without losing functionality

**Preference Cookies:**
- Remember your settings and preferences
- Store language, theme, and display settings
- Improve your experience by personalizing content

**Performance/Analytics Cookies:**
- Collect information about how you use Services
- Help us understand user behavior and platform performance
- Measure effectiveness of features and content
- Allow us to identify bugs and technical issues

**Advertising/Marketing Cookies:**
- Enable targeted advertising on our site and others
- Track your interests and browsing behavior
- Allow advertisers to show relevant ads
- Enable conversion tracking for marketing campaigns

**Social Media Cookies:**
- Allow sharing to social media platforms
- Enable social login integration
- Track social media referrals and interactions

### 7.2 Other Tracking Technologies

**Web Beacons/Pixels:**
- Transparent images embedded in pages or emails
- Confirm page views and email opens
- Track when you take specific actions
- Measure advertising effectiveness

**Local Storage:**
- Browser storage mechanisms similar to cookies
- Store larger amounts of data than cookies
- Persist until manually deleted
- Not sent to servers with each request

**Log Files:**
- Server-side records of all requests
- Capture IP address, browser type, pages visited
- Record timestamps and referrer information
- Retained per our data retention policy

**Device Fingerprinting:**
- Collection of device characteristics to create unique identifier
- Used to detect fraud and prevent abuse
- May be combined with other data for identification

**Analytics Pixels/Tags:**
- Code that tracks user behavior across platforms
- Enables cross-site tracking (with your consent)
- Used for marketing and behavioral analysis
- Retained per our data retention policy

### 7.3 Managing Your Cookie Preferences

**Browser Settings:**
- Most browsers allow you to accept or decline cookies
- You can set preferences for different cookie types
- You can delete existing cookies anytime
- You can restrict third-party cookies
